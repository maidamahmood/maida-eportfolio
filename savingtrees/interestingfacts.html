1-An average size tree produces enough oxygen in one year to keep a family of four breathing.
2-Three trees planted in the right place around buildings can cut air-conditioning costs up to 50 percent.
3-No other organism on Earth lives as long as a tree.
4-The biggest tree is a giant sequoia in California’s Redwood Forest. It measures some 30 stories tall and 82’ in circumference.
5-Trees help improve our water quality as they slow and filter rainfall
6-“Moon trees” were grown from seeds taken to the moon in early 1971.
7-Earth has more than 60,000 known tree species.
8-More than half of all tree species exist only in a single country.
9-The first known tree was a leafless, fern-like plant from New York.
10-Trees in a forest can 'talk' and share nutrients through an underground internet built by soil fungi.
